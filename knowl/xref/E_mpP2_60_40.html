Exercise 3.4.6. The \((60, 40)\) strategy for Player 2.

Suppose Player 2 plays H 60% of the time and T 40% of the time.
  1. What are \(P_2(H)\) and \(P_2(T)\text{?}\)
  2. What do you think Player 1 should do? Does this differ from your ideal mixed strategy in Exercise 3.4.4? Explain.
  3. We can use expected value to compute what Player 1 should do in response to Player 2’s 60/40 strategy. First, consider a pure strategy for Player 1. Compute the expected value for Player 1 if she only plays H while Player 2 plays H with probability .6 and T with probability .4. This expected value is \(E_1(H)\text{,}\) above.
  4. Similarly, compute the expected value for Player 1 if she plays only T (call it \(E_1(T)\)).
  5. Which pure strategy has a higher expected value for Player 1? If Player 1 plays this pure strategy, will she do better than your predicted value of the game?
