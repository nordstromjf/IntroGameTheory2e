Activity 3.4.9. Equations for Player 1’s expected values.

Let \(P_2(H)\) and \(P_2(T)\) be the probabilities that Player 2 plays H and T respectively.

(a)

Find equations for \(E_1(H)\) and \(E_1(T)\) in terms of \(P_2(H)\) and \(P_2(T)\) for the game of Matching Pennies.
Hint.
The expected value, \(E_1(H)\text{,}\) is (Player 1’s payoff for [H, H]\(\times\) the probability Player 2 plays H)\(+\) (Player 1’s payoff for [H, T]\(\times\) the probability Player 2 plays T).

(b)

Since we want \(E_1(H)=E_1(T)\text{,}\) set your two equations equal to each other. This gives you one equation in terms of \(P_2(H)\) and \(P_2(T)\text{.}\)

(c)

Explain why we must also have the equation \(P_2(H)+P_2(T)=1\text{.}\)
